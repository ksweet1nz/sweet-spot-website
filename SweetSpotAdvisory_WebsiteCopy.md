### The Model

## How it works

Every engagement runs for three months. There is a fixed fee, three equal payments, and a hard stop at the end. The goal is straightforward. You end the engagement with systems that work, a team that knows how to use them, and no need for ongoing outside help.

I assess what technology your business has, identify what is working and what is not, implement fixes and integrations, train the team, document everything, and leave. This is not a retainer. It is not a subscription. It is a defined piece of work with a beginning, a middle, and an end.

---

### Four Phases (Expanded)

## Four phases. Twelve weeks.

**1. Audit** — Weeks 1–2
What you have, what it costs, what it does and does not do. I map every tool, every licence, every integration point. The output is an honest current-state picture not a sales document for more work. You get a clear view of where you stand. This is where I find the unused licences, the duplicate systems, and the manual processes that should have been automated years ago. The deliverable is an honest picture of your technology. Its written down, specific, and yours to keep regardless of what comes next.

**2. Prioritise** — Weeks 2–3
Agree on outcomes. What gets fixed, what gets cut, what gets built. No work begins until priorities are clear and both sides have agreed on what success looks like. This phase is where the business problem drives the technology decisions, not the other way around. You will know exactly what the engagement will deliver before a single hour of implementation work begins.

**3. Build** — Weeks 3–11
Implement, integrate, train, measure. This is the longest phase because it is where the real work happens. I connect systems that currently operate in isolation, automate manual and repetitive processes, rationalise software spend, build visibility and reporting that does not currently exist, digitise internal workflows, and lay the groundwork for AI where the foundation supports it. Your team is involved throughout. This is knowledge transfer, not a black box. You will see progress as it happens, not a finished product that appears at the end.

**4. Hand over** — Week 12
Documented, trained, independent. Everything I have built is handed over with documentation clear enough that someone else could maintain it tomorrow. Thirty and ninety day check-ins are included. After that you are on your own — which is the whole point.

### The Hard Stop

## The hard stop

Every engagement ends at three months. This is not a policy for the sake of policy. It is the core of how this works.

Most advisory relationships drift. The scope expands. The timeline extends. The dependency grows. Before long, the outside help has become a permanent cost centre that the business cannot operate without. That is the opposite of what I am trying to do.

A fixed end date forces clarity. It forces prioritisation. It means I focus on what matters most and build it in a way that your team can own. If three months is not enough time to make a meaningful difference, the problem is the approach not the timeline.

For you this means every hour of the engagement is focused on building something that lasts not on extending the relationship. The incentive is always to finish well, not to find reasons to continue.

Due to the personall nature of the engagement, I can only run three engagements per year. By application.
