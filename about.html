Founder

Kevin Sweet

Thirty years inside technology. One practice built around fixing what others leave broken.

Technology Advisor & Founder, Sweet Spot Advisory

Kevin Sweet has spent more than 30 years working inside technology programs at organisations ranging from government agencies and global property developers to early-stage SaaS companies and mid-market businesses. He has held senior roles at Lendlease, where he led a platform that secured over $70M in external funding, and at Greyhound Racing NSW, where he built the entire technology function from scratch. He has run teams of 40 people across five countries and worked as the sole technical driver in environments where nothing existed before he arrived.

Across all of it, the same pattern kept appearing. Businesses were spending real money on technology and not getting real returns. Software was being bought without a clear problem to solve. Systems were accumulating without anyone connecting them to how the business actually worked. The people responsible for the technology were often skilled at the tools but removed from the commercial decisions, and the people making the commercial decisions rarely understood what the technology could or could not do. That gap is where the problems lived. Sweet Spot Advisory exists to close it.

Sweet Spot Advisory is not an IT contractor and it is not a management consulting firm. Kevin comes in for a fixed term of three months, works through the technology stack alongside the team, fixes what is broken, documents what exists and trains the people who will be there when he leaves. The engagement ends with a business that understands its own systems and can run them without outside help. No retainer. No dependency. No reason to come back unless there is a new problem worth solving.

The approach

The approach

Your technology problems are almost never about the technology. They are about what the business needed and what got bought instead. Starting with the business problem — not the tool — is what makes the difference between a system that works and one that gets paid for but never used.

You will not receive a report and a handshake. Everything recommended gets built. Every outcome gets measured. And your team will know how to run what has been built so you are never dependent on a call to keep things working.

Honest about AI — it earns its place when the foundation is right, not before.

Tools don't transform businesses. People with the right tools do.
